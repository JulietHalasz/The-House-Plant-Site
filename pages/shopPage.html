The Houseplant Site

  • Hoya

    From £13

    Hoya plants are evergreen climbers suitable for the conservatory, bearing pendant heads of waxy flowers which can be highly scented, particularly in the evening. Hoyas are native to Indo-China, Indonesia and Australasia, in this country they need to be grown in a warm bright room.

  • Devil's Ivy

    From £15

    Devil’s ivy, golden pathos, ceylon creeper, or hunter’s robe. It has so many names because it’s one of the most popular houseplants in the world. There are two reasons for that - it’s extremely versatile. It’s a fantastic hanging plant, looks great on a mantelpiece or shelf, and it can even be trained up a wall. It’s also incredibly easy to care for. It loves rooms with lots of moisture, isn’t fussy about light conditions, and is super simple to water.

  • Corn Plant

    From £11

    While corn plants can grow as high as six metres in the wild, indoors they are more about character than size, providing a bit of tropical flavour even on grey days. To keep them happy remember: be generous with the misting, sparing with the watering. It will also love it if you give it a feed with liquid fertiliser once a month in spring and summer. Corn plants are happy in anything from light shade to soft bright light, but keep them out of harsh direct sun.
